About the Cover
In the album cover for “Old Sport”: The Gatsby Mixtape, there are many different depictions of motifs from the novel, The Great Gatsby by F. Scott Fitzgerald, that come together to explain the different tracks that we’ve created. The track featured on the cover is “Old Sport”. This track is a reference to Gatsby’s past life and struggle working his way up to the top that will help him transition into his life as a millionaire. For example, the track features references to his time with the late Dan Cody, his time in the army, and his journey to reclaim Daisy.

The first symbol represented on the cover is the green light in the top left hand corner. The green light is a symbol of Gatsby’s journey to wooing Daisy back into his life and away from the life she has created with her husband Tom Buchanan. Throughout the novel, Gatsby would look out to this light across the bay from his dock, strengthening his drive and desire for Daisy to fall in with him again. It is seen though that when this light fades and eventually goes out, it results in Gatsby’s downfall. Because of of that downfall and the role in plays in the novel, it was a significant enough symbol to include on the cover.

Additionally, incorporating the color symbolism, there is the representation of East and West Egg. In the novel, East Egg was a representation of old wealth, which is where Daisy and Tom lived, while West Egg was a representation of new wealth, where Nick and Gatsby lived. Both “eggs” are drawn as cracked to exemplify the corruption that seeps into both areas that are only separated by the Valley of Ashes, an area of poverty. The Valley of Ashes juxtaposes the elaborate and elegant lifestyle that all those who live in East and West Egg take for granted.

The quote, “So we beat on, boats against the current, borne back ceaselessly into the past” was also included in the bottom portion of the album cover. Fitzgerald added this quote at the end of the novel as a reminder of how Americans should try to continue to move forward. Even though the American dream can become obsessive and corrupt, you have to find a way forward once your dream comes crashing down. This quote plays an integral role in the song “Old Sport” as it is a representation of Gatsby’s life story.

Another symbol that is from Gatsby’s past is the wing pin badge that represents his time at sea with Dan Cody. Without meeting Mr. Cody, Gatsby would’ve never came in contact with the resources and funds that blossomed his millionaire lifestyle.

Lastly, there are the eyes and glasses of Dr. TJ Eckleburg, that in the novel, are located in the Valley of Ashes, constantly watching over people as they move through the Valley of Ashes headed towards New York City. They are added to show how each character’s actions were being watched at any given point in time and depicting the lack of morals that was present within each character.

Overall, the inclusion of the green light, East and West Egg, the quote, the wing pin, and the glasses all come together to give more meaning behind “Old Sport”: The Gatsby Mixtape. Each track incorporates a different element in some shape or form to bring together the story of all the characters’ lives in The Great Gatsby.
